# Glossary

**d12**: a twelve-sided die.

**3d12**: three d12 rolled together, the base of every roll.

**Keep 3**: from the dice you roll, you keep three and sum them. With no Advantage or
Disadvantage you roll exactly three and keep all three.

**Modifier (mod)**: a number added to a roll. A roll adds one Characteristic and one Aptitude,
and nothing else.

**Characteristic**: a character's core modifier for a broad capability, from 0 to 20. One is
added to every roll.

**Aptitude**: a skill-area modifier, from -1 to 10, where 0 is the untrained baseline. One is
added to every roll alongside a Characteristic.

**Advantage (Av)**: a bonus die. For each Advantage, roll one extra d12 and keep the best three.

**Disadvantage (Dv)**: a penalty die. For each Disadvantage, roll one extra d12 and keep the
worst three. Advantage and Disadvantage cancel one for one.

**Target**: the number a roll must meet or beat: an Armor Class, a Difficulty, or an opponent's
total.

**Success / Failure**: a roll meets or beats its target, or it does not.

**Critical success**: the three kept dice all show 12. The result is the best version of what
was attempted, plus a perk; on an attack it also doubles damage.

**Critical failure**: the three kept dice all show 1. The GM chooses a bad turn of events.

**DC (Difficulty)**: the target for an Ability roll or a Saving throw, set by the GM from a
reference table.

**AC (Armor Class)**: the target for an Attack roll, a number on each creature's sheet.

**HP**: how much damage a creature takes before it drops.

**Energy**: the resource spent to power skills; more Energy spent means more damage dice.

**Memory**: the slots that hold a character's equipped skills.

**XP, Attunement, Training**: the currencies of growth. XP buys skills; Attunement buys HP,
Energy, and Memory; Training buys Characteristics and Aptitudes.
