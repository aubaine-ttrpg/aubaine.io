# Armor Class

## What it is

A creature's Armor Class (AC) is the target an Attack roll must meet or beat. It sits on the
creature's sheet.

## How it works

Take the wearer's value in the armour's scaling characteristic, divide by the armour's Per and
round down, then add the Base. Some armour also imposes Disadvantage on certain rolls. Anyone can
wear anything; there is no proficiency.

## Armours

| Armour | Class | Base | Scales from | Per | Penalties |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Unclothed | none | 18 | Dexterity | 1 | none |
| Leather | light | 19 | Dexterity | 1 | none |
| Mail | medium | 22 | Dexterity | 2 | none |
| Plate | heavy | 26 | Strength | 2 | Dv on Saving throws and Dexterity rolls |
| Noble's Wear | light | 19 | Charisma | 2 | none |

## Limits

The GM can set or override any creature's AC.

## At the table

> **GM:** You swing at the mailed sergeant. Strength and Blades.
> **Player:** 3d12: 9, 7, 3, plus Strength 6, plus Blades 3. Twenty-eight.
> **GM:** The blade bites through the mail.

## Design Notes

Because AC is built from a characteristic, it rises as characters grow, so a stronger world
fields tougher defences without a separate scaling table. Heavier armour trades a Disadvantage
for a higher base, and scaling from different characteristics lets a build reach the same
defence by different routes, including one a spellcaster or a commander already invests in.
