# Advantage & Disadvantage

## What it is

Advantage (Av) and Disadvantage (Dv) are how circumstance bends a roll: a good angle, the right
tool, a treacherous floor. They are the only situational lever; there are no flat bonuses.

## How it works

1. For each Advantage roll one extra d12; for each Disadvantage roll one extra d12.
2. Advantage and Disadvantage cancel one for one. Total them into a single net first.
3. With net Advantage, keep the **best** three dice. With net Disadvantage, keep the **worst**
   three. With neither, keep all three.
4. Add your modifiers and compare as usual.

Advantage and Disadvantage come only from equipped skills and the GM.

## Limits

The first step of Advantage or Disadvantage moves the odds most; each further step adds less.
The full curve is computed in the lab.

## At the table

> **GM:** You have braced the shot on the rampart. Advantage.
> **Player:** 4d12: 2, 11, 8, 12. Keep the best three, 11, 8, 12, for thirty-one, plus modifiers.

## Design Notes

Circumstance adds dice instead of flat numbers, so it bends the odds without inflating the
target scale. Cancelling before rolling keeps piles of dice off the table. Returns diminish on
purpose: one Advantage is a real gift, a fourth barely shows.
