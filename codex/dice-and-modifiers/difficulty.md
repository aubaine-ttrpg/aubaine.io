# Difficulty

## What it is

The target numbers for Ability rolls and Saving throws. A reference the GM reads, and can
override.

## How it works

Pick the band that fits the fiction and use its number as the target. A character's modifiers
are added to the roll, so a given band gets easier as they grow.

| Band | DC |
| --- | --- |
| Easy | 14 |
| Routine | 17 |
| Standard | 19 |
| Hard | 23 |
| Formidable | 26 |
| Heroic | 29 |
| Legendary | 32 |

Do not call for a roll a character cannot meaningfully fail. Reserve the hardest bands for
moments worth an Advantage or careful setup.

Keep the DC to yourself. Announcing it is optional and best avoided: an unknown target keeps the
roll tense.

## At the table

> **GM:** The ledge is wet and narrow. Dexterity and Athletics to cross.
> **Player:** 3d12: 8, 7, 3, plus Dexterity 5, plus Athletics 2. Twenty-five.
> **GM:** You reach the far side.

## Design Notes

The numbers are flat, not scaled to the character, so growth shows as a rising modifier clearing
fixed bands: a task that once demanded a strong roll later becomes routine, and that is growth
the player can see. For a powerful party the GM raises the challenge by choosing a higher band,
not by inflating every number.
