# The Dice

## What it is

Aubaine runs on the twelve-sided die (d12). Every roll starts with three of them: 3d12.

## How it works

1. Roll 3d12.
2. Read the dice and keep three. With no Advantage or Disadvantage, you keep all three.
3. Sum the kept dice. That sum is the core of your result, before modifiers.

The kept sum runs from 3 (three 1s) to 36 (three 12s) and centres on 19 to 20.

## At the table

> **GM:** Roll it.
> **Player:** 3d12: 9, 4, 11. Twenty-four, before my modifiers.

## Design Notes

Three d12 make a bell curve: results cluster near the centre and the extremes are rare, so a
roll is predictable enough to plan around and can still surprise. Keeping three dice, rather
than summing everything rolled, is what lets Advantage and Disadvantage add dice without
changing the scale of the result.
