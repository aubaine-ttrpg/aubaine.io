# Resolution

## What it is

The one procedure that settles any uncertain action, in a fight or out of it.

## How it works

1. Roll 3d12 and keep the best three.
2. Add one Characteristic.
3. Add one Aptitude.
4. Compare the total to the target. Meet or beat it to succeed.

The GM names the Characteristic and the Aptitude that fit the action. Nothing else is added:
circumstance is handled by Advantage and Disadvantage, not by flat bonuses.

## Outcomes

- **Success**: the total meets or beats the target.
- **Failure**: it does not. The GM says how the situation shifts. A miss costs time, ground, or
  a resource, or it turns the scene; it never just stalls.
- **Critical success**: the three kept dice all show 12.
- **Critical failure**: the three kept dice all show 1.

## At the table

> **GM:** The elf is stonewalling you. Give me Charisma and Persuasion.
> **Player:** 3d12: 8, 11, 3. Nineteen, plus Charisma 6, plus Persuasion 4. Twenty-nine.
> **GM:** He folds. "Fine. We do this my way."

## Design Notes

One Characteristic plus one Aptitude is the entire modifier stack, with no situational pluses to
add up, which keeps targets meaningful and the arithmetic fast. The system has no partial band,
so failure is written to move play: the GM makes a miss cost or change something rather than
leave the scene untouched.
