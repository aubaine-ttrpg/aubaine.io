# Characteristics

## What it is

A character's core modifiers: what they are naturally capable of. They are modifiers, not
scores, so the number is what you add, and each runs from 0 to 20.

## The characteristics

| Characteristic | Covers |
| --- | --- |
| Strength (STR) | lifting, breaking, grappling, heavy melee |
| Dexterity (DEX) | dodging, aim, balance, stealth, fine handwork |
| Endurance (END) | resisting poison, exhaustion, cold, and pain |
| Intelligence (INT) | lore, deduction, planning, reading a situation |
| Spirit (SPI) | resolve, perception, instinct, composure |
| Charisma (CHA) | persuasion, command, deception, performance |

## How it works

Every roll adds one Characteristic, chosen to fit the action.

## At the table

> **GM:** You haul the portcullis up by hand. Strength and Athletics.
> **Player:** 3d12: 8, 6, 5. Nineteen, plus Strength 9, plus Athletics 2. Thirty.

## Design Notes

Characteristics are kept few and non-overlapping, so each owns a clear slice of what a body or
mind can do. Capping the modifier at 20 bounds the top of the range, keeping targets within
reach of the dice.
