# Roll Types

## What it is

The roll types, each named for the target you compare your total against.

## How it works

Resolve every one as a normal roll (3d12 keep three, plus one Characteristic and one Aptitude).
They differ only in the target:

- **Attack roll**: beat the target's Armor Class. Against several targets at once, use the
  highest AC.
- **Saving throw**: beat the Difficulty of the effect you are resisting.
- **Ability roll**: beat a Difficulty the GM sets from the fiction.
- **Opposed roll**: both sides roll; the higher total wins. On a tie, the higher modifier wins,
  and a critical wins outright.

## Outcomes

Meet or beat the target to succeed. Criticals apply as usual; on an attack roll a critical also
doubles the damage.

## At the table

> **GM:** The thug lunges to shove you off the cart. Opposed: Strength and Athletics, both sides.
> **Player:** 3d12: 9, 8, 4, plus Strength 6, plus Athletics 1. Twenty-eight.
> **GM:** He rolls twenty-four. You plant your feet and throw him off.

## Design Notes

Opposed rolls swing more than checks because two rolls stack their spread, so a small edge is
only a small favourite, which suits a real contest. Resolving ties by the higher modifier, then
a critical, avoids re-rolls and rewards the more capable side.
