# No-AI-Tell Style Guide

## Goal

The output must read like finished tabletop material, not generated copy.

Avoid generic hype, summary padding, repeated structure, and vague fantasy words.

## Forbidden Phrases

Do not use these unless quoting user text for critique:

- delve into
- embark on
- unleash
- rich tapestry
- vibrant world
- secrets await
- ancient evil stirs
- looming threat
- shrouded in mystery
- adds depth and intrigue
- dynamic and immersive
- compelling narrative
- unique and memorable
- thrilling adventure
- at its core
- serves as
- designed to
- players will find themselves
- whether you are a seasoned veteran
- perfect for any campaign
- more than just
- not only... but also
- in this adventure, players will
- a world where
- filled with mystery and danger

## Replacement Rules

### Replace hype with table facts

Bad:
“This encounter creates a thrilling tactical challenge.”

Good:
“The bridge collapses in three rounds. The archers cut the ropes if anyone reaches the middle span.”

### Replace vague stakes with concrete stakes

Bad:
“The town faces a terrible danger.”

Good:
“At dawn, the bailiff hangs six miners for a murder committed by the mayor’s son.”

### Replace atmosphere-only lore with playable detail

Bad:
“The ruins have a dark history.”

Good:
“Every statue has its hands broken off. The missing hands are nailed above the priest’s door.”

### Replace abstract NPCs with motives

Bad:
“She is morally complex.”

Good:
“She sold the road wardens to the goblins because the wardens left her brother to die.”

### Replace generic mystery with evidence

Bad:
“The truth is hidden in the manor.”

Good:
“The nursery mirror reflects the room as it looked the night of the murder.”

## Sentence-Level Tests

Delete any sentence that does not provide one of these:

- A thing characters can notice
- A thing characters can do
- A thing that can happen
- A rule trigger
- A consequence
- A cost
- A reward
- A motive
- A clue
- A table-facing instruction
- A safety or accessibility note

## Tone Rules

Use nouns and verbs before adjectives.

Prefer:

- “The lock bleeds when picked.”
- “The guard wants the captain embarrassed, not dead.”
- “The shrine answers only questions spoken by a liar.”

Avoid:

- “eerie atmosphere”
- “sinister presence”
- “haunting ambience”
- “deeply immersive experience”

## Meta-Language Ban

Do not say:

- “Here is content generated for you”
- “This should give your players...”
- “This is designed to...”
- “I made sure to...”
- “As an AI...”

Instead, provide the content.

## Final Cleanup Pass

Before output, scan for:

- Repeated sentence openings
- Generic fantasy adjectives
- Empty transition phrases
- Explaining the design instead of using it
- Overlong descriptions
- Mechanics without numbers or procedures
- “May” or “might” where a definite table fact is better
- Unclear pronoun references
- Any mention of AI unless explicitly requested
