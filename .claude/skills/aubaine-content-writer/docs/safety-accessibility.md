# Safety and Accessibility Practices

## Safety Goal

Make the table safer without weakening the game.

Do not use player discomfort as entertainment.

## When to Include Safety Notes

Include brief safety notes when content involves:

- Horror
- Torture
- Sexual content
- Abuse
- Self-harm
- Harm to children
- Bigotry
- Slavery
- Addiction
- Domestic violence
- Body horror
- Psychological manipulation
- Real-world trauma analogues

## Safety Tools

### Session Zero

Use before a campaign or intense one-shot.

Cover:

- Tone
- Genre
- Expected themes
- Lines
- Veils
- Player goals
- Character boundaries
- PvP expectations
- Romance expectations
- Lethality
- Safety tools
- Scheduling and attendance

### Lines and Veils

Line: content that does not appear.

Veil: content may exist but fades to black or stays offscreen.

Use lines and veils before play and revise them as needed.

### X-Card or Equivalent

Use a clear pause/edit tool.

A player can signal discomfort. The group rewinds, skips, changes, or removes the content.

Do not require explanation.

### Script Change Language

Use simple table controls:

- Pause
- Rewind
- Fast-forward
- Frame-by-frame
- Resume

### Open Door

Players may step away without explanation.

## Writing Sensitive Content

Prefer implication over graphic detail.

Write consequences, choices, and tone without lingering on harm.

Give alternate versions when the premise can work without the sensitive element.

Do not surprise the table with extreme content.

## Accessibility Goal

Make content easier to read, search, print, skim, and use aloud.

## Text Accessibility Rules

Use:

- Clear heading hierarchy
- Short paragraphs
- Simple sentence structure for rules
- Defined terms
- Consistent labels
- Adequate spacing
- Tables only when useful
- Player-facing summaries
- Plain-language safety notes

Avoid:

- Dense lore blocks
- Tiny font assumptions
- Color-only distinctions
- Hidden rules inside flavor text
- Multi-column reading order that breaks screen readers
- Decorative symbols that carry essential meaning
- Excessive italics
- Heavy background texture behind text

## Table Design Rules

Good random table entries create action.

Each entry should include at least one of:

- Threat
- Clue
- Choice
- Cost
- Opportunity
- NPC action
- Faction movement
- Resource change
- Timer movement
- Location change

## Handout Design Rules

Player handouts should be:

- Short
- Diegetic when useful
- Clear enough to act on
- Legible without GM explanation
- Free of essential information hidden in decorative prose

## Inclusive Design Rules

Avoid making real-world identities inherently evil, cursed, monstrous, primitive, or biologically destined for moral behavior.

Avoid disability-as-punishment tropes.

Use cultures, factions, faiths, and species with internal variety.

For villain groups, define ideology, actions, leadership, and material incentives rather than essential nature.

## Final Safety Check

Before output, ask silently:

- Does sensitive material need a content note?
- Is any harm described more graphically than needed?
- Can the GM adjust or remove the content?
- Are safety tools brief and practical?
- Does the text avoid harmful essentialism?

## Final Accessibility Check

Before output, ask silently:

- Can this be skimmed mid-session?
- Are headings clear?
- Are terms consistent?
- Are rules separated from flavor?
- Does the content work without color?
- Can a GM read key text aloud easily?
