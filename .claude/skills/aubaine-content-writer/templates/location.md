# Location Template

## First Impression

What characters notice immediately.

## Purpose

Why this place exists in play.

## Interactive Features

Objects, terrain, mechanisms, evidence, hazards, doors, tools, or rituals.

## Inhabitants

Who is here and what they are doing.

## Clues

What can be learned here.

## Dangers

What can hurt, trap, expose, delay, or mislead characters.

## Rewards

Treasure, access, information, allies, shortcuts, resources.

## Change Over Time

What happens if characters wait, make noise, take something, or return later.

## Connections

Where exits, rumors, tracks, maps, tunnels, roads, or relationships lead.
