# Example: System-Neutral One-Shot Seed

## Premise

A flooded bell tower rings every night for a god the town executed last winter.

## Hook

The reeve pays 80 silver to stop the bell before the spring market opens in three days. Merchants will not enter while the dead god's name echoes over the road.

## Situation

The town killed a river spirit after blaming it for a fever. The fever came from the tannery upstream. The spirit's drowned priest now rings the bell to call the river over the town.

## Stakes

- If the characters act, they can expose the tannery and bargain with the priest.
- If they delay, the lower streets flood.
- If they fail, the river takes the market square and the old bridge.

## Strong Start

At midnight, the bell rings underwater. Fish spill from chimneys. A child points at the tower and says, “The man with no lungs is climbing again.”

## Key NPCs

### Reeve Salla Dorn

- Want: Save the market and her office.
- Fear: The tannery records becoming public.
- Secret: She signed the order to execute the spirit.
- Leverage: Can open the town archive.
- If ignored: Pays mercenaries to burn the tower.

### Drowned Priest Keval

- Want: Public confession and burial rites for the spirit.
- Fear: Being forgotten when the river recedes.
- Secret: He can stop the flood but not forgive the town alone.
- Leverage: Controls the bell and the waterlogged dead.
- If ignored: Rings the seventh bell and breaks the bridge.

## Required Conclusions and Clues

| Conclusion | Clue 1 | Clue 2 | Clue 3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| The tannery caused the fever. | Dead fish collect below its drain. | Archive records show sickness began downstream. | A tanner has the same rash as the fever victims. |
| The spirit was executed unjustly. | The trial transcript has missing witness names. | Old villagers remember soldiers blocking testimony. | The spirit's shrine contains offerings dated after the first fever death. |
| Keval wants confession, not slaughter. | He avoids harming children. | His bell pattern matches a funeral rite. | He leaves the reeve's house flooded but intact. |

## Countdown

1. Night one: wells taste of silt.
2. Night two: cellars flood and dead minnows spell names.
3. Night three: the market square sinks knee-deep.
4. Seventh bell: the river changes course through town.

## Possible Endings

- Confession: The reeve admits the cover-up; Keval stops the bell.
- Bargain: The town restores the shrine and pays blood money.
- Violence: Keval is destroyed; the river remains poisoned.
- Failure: The town survives on stilts and blames outsiders.
