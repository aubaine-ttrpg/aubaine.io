# Quality Gates

Run these silently before final output.

## Universal Gate

- The output answers the request.
- The output is table-ready.
- The first useful content appears quickly.
- Assumptions are minimal and reasonable.
- No AI or generation language appears unless requested.
- No generic hype appears.
- No required detail is vague.
- No section exists only to sound impressive.

## Writing Gate

- Sentences are short where rules need clarity.
- Descriptions are concrete.
- Key terms are consistent.
- Repeated phrasing is removed.
- Filler transitions are removed.
- Flavor text does not hide mechanics.
- Boxed/read-aloud text describes only what characters can perceive.

## Playability Gate

- The GM knows what is happening.
- Players have something clear to do.
- Stakes are concrete.
- Choices have consequences.
- Waiting changes the situation.
- NPCs and factions act if ignored.
- Rewards matter.
- Endings are flexible.

## Mystery Gate

- Required conclusions have at least three clues.
- Required clues are not locked behind a single roll.
- Clues appear in different forms.
- Suspects have means, motive, opportunity, and pressure points.
- Wrong theories create complications, not dead ends.

## Mechanics Gate

- Trigger is clear.
- Procedure is clear.
- Roll or resource is specified.
- Success result is clear.
- Partial result is clear when the system uses one.
- Failure consequence is clear.
- Cost or limit is clear.
- System math is respected.
- Existing options are not overshadowed.

## Encounter Gate

- Objective is clear.
- Opposition has a goal.
- Terrain or constraints matter.
- At least one non-obvious tactic exists.
- The encounter can end in more than one way.
- Failure changes the situation.

## Dungeon Gate

- Map structure supports choices.
- Rooms have interactive elements.
- Empty rooms have purpose.
- Danger is telegraphed.
- Time, noise, light, or wandering pressure matters when appropriate.
- Factions or monsters move.
- Treasure creates risk or consequences.

## Safety Gate

- Sensitive material is signposted.
- Graphic detail is no stronger than needed.
- Lines, veils, or opt-out tools are included when useful.
- Harmful stereotypes are avoided.
- Mature content is purposeful.

## Accessibility Gate

- Headings are clear.
- Dense paragraphs are broken up.
- Tables are simple.
- Color is not the only information channel.
- Player handouts are readable.
- The GM can scan the material under time pressure.

## Final Cut Gate

Remove anything that is:

- Decorative only
- Redundant
- Abstract
- Self-congratulatory
- Over-explained
- Not useful at the table
