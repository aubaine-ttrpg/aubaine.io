# Citations and standards used by this skill

Use these as the starting reference set for visual support design, PDF/export production, accessibility, print/POD, legal, and code-quality guidance. Always check the current platform, marketplace, or printer requirements before final export because digital and POD specifications change.

## PDF, output production, and print/POD

1. PDF Association, “Technical side and requirements of PDF/X.” Explains PDF/X output intents and standardized exchange goals. https://pdfa.org/technical-side-and-requirements-of-pdfx/
2. PDF Association, “PDF/X in a Nutshell.” Concise overview of PDF/X and output intents. https://pdfa.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/05/PDFX-in-a-Nutshell.pdf
3. Ghent Workgroup, “Commercial Print.” Notes modern commercial-print specifications based on ISO PDF/X-4 and legacy PDF/X-1a-based specifications. https://gwg.org/commercial-print/
4. Ghent Workgroup, “PDF/X workflow.” Practical workflow context for GWG/PDF/X. https://gwg.org/pdf-x-workflow/
5. Ghent Workgroup, “Digital Print Specification.” Standardized guidelines for small-format digital print PDF creation and quality control. https://gwg.org/technical-specifications/digitalprintspecification/
6. Adobe, “Produce print-ready PDF files in InDesign.” Guidance on links, fonts, bleeds/margins, colors, and preflight before export. https://helpx.adobe.com/indesign/desktop/print/print-production-and-file-creation/produce-print-ready-pdf-files.html
7. Adobe Acrobat Pro, “Output intents in PDFs.” Explains output intent as the final reproduction/color destination. https://helpx.adobe.com/acrobat/using/output-intents-pdfs-acrobat-pro.html
8. DriveThruRPG Partners, “Quick Specifications for Print Books.” POD-specific guidance for bleed, safety, margins, and crop marks. https://help.drivethrupartners.com/hc/en-us/articles/12780800178583-Quick-Specifications-for-Print-Books
9. Lulu, “Full Bleed instructions for print-ready PDFs.” POD-specific bleed guidance: 0.125 in / 3.18 mm past trim. https://help.lulu.com/en/support/solutions/articles/64000255584-what-is-full-bleed-
10. Lulu Blog, “How to Make a Print-Ready PDF for Your Book.” Practical guidance on bleed, color, and embedded fonts. https://blog.lulu.com/how-to-make-a-print-ready-pdf/
11. Lulu, “Tips for Formatting Documents.” Guidance that fonts must be embedded in print-ready PDFs. https://help.lulu.com/en/support/solutions/articles/64000255583-tips-for-formatting-documents

## Accessibility, contrast, screen PDFs, and readable print

12. W3C, “Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.2.” Current WCAG recommendation. https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG22/
13. W3C WAI, “Understanding Success Criterion 1.4.3: Contrast (Minimum).” 4.5:1 normal text and 3:1 large text thresholds. https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/contrast-minimum.html
14. W3C WAI, “Understanding Success Criterion 1.4.11: Non-text Contrast.” Non-text contrast guidance. https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG21/Understanding/non-text-contrast.html
15. W3C WAI, “Understanding Success Criterion 1.4.5: Images of Text.” Prefer real text over rasterized images of text. https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/images-of-text.html
16. PDF Association, “PDF/UA.” PDF/UA / ISO 14289 accessibility overview. https://pdfa.org/resource/pdfua-flyer/
17. PDF Association, “ISO 14289-1.” Notes that Tagged PDF carries semantic information needed for accessibility. https://pdfa.org/resource/iso-14289-pdfua/
18. PDF Association, “Tagged PDF Best Practice Guide: Syntax.” Developer guidance for tagged PDF/PDF/UA. https://pdfa.org/resource/tagged-pdf-best-practice-guide-syntax/
19. Adobe Acrobat Pro, “Creating accessible PDFs.” Acrobat accessibility checking and remediation guidance, including forms, alternate text, tab order, and tags. https://helpx.adobe.com/acrobat/using/creating-accessible-pdfs.html
20. Adobe Acrobat Pro, “Reading Order tool for PDFs.” Guidance on reading order, figure tags, and alt text. https://helpx.adobe.com/acrobat/using/touch-reading-order-tool-pdfs.html
21. British Dyslexia Association, “Dyslexia Style Guide.” Readability guidance on fonts, sizes, spacing, and contrast. https://www.thedyslexia-spldtrust.org.uk/media/downloads/69-bda-style-guide-april14.pdf
22. RNIB, “Clear print guidance 2023.” Guidance on print size, spacing, and clear print. https://www.rnib.org.uk/documents/1643/Clear_Print_guidance_2023.docx
23. Round Table on Information Access for People with Print Disabilities, “Guidelines for Producing Clear Print.” Guidance on headings, columns, line spacing, and print readability. https://printdisability.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/Guidelines-for-Producing-Clear-Print-2022-PDF-FINAL.pdf
24. Wired, “Tabletop RPG Rule Books Can Be Beautiful and Accessible.” Industry-facing discussion of TTRPG accessibility, navigable rulebooks, screen readers, dyslexia-friendly layout, redundant color cues, and alternate accessible editions. https://www.wired.com/story/ttrpg-accessible-design
25. Kumar and Wang, “Uncovering the New Accessibility Crisis in Scholarly PDFs.” Research on PDF accessibility failures and the importance of tagging, alt text, table headers, and tab order. https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.03022
26. Schmitt-Koopmann et al., “Towards More Accessible Scientific PDFs for People with Visual Impairments.” Research on improving PDF tag accuracy through remediation workflows. https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.22216
