# Legal, licensing, and attribution hygiene

This file is not legal advice. It is a production checklist to reduce obvious publishing mistakes.

## Track every rights source

Create a rights ledger before layout:

- Game text written for this project.
- Open game content / licensed SRD material.
- Third-party mechanics and compatibility references.
- Fonts and embedding rights.
- Art, maps, icons, textures, borders, stock assets.
- Public-domain material and proof of status.
- Creative Commons assets and required attribution.
- Playtest quotes, endorsements, and testimonials.

## Open RPG licenses

- If using D&D SRD material, use the current official SRD and attribution requirements. D&D Beyond states SRD 5.1 and 5.2 are available under CC BY 4.0 with attribution; see citation 29.
- For CC BY 4.0 materials, give credit, provide the license link, and indicate if changes were made; see citation 30.
- For ORC-licensed materials, use the official ORC license text and attribution mechanics; see citations 31-32.
- Do not mix license regimes casually. If content comes from OGL, ORC, CC, public domain, or proprietary permissions, document it line by line.

## Compatibility claims

Avoid implying affiliation, endorsement, or official compatibility unless the license allows it and notices are correct.

Safer wording pattern:

> Compatible with [system/version] under [license/source]. This product is independent and is not affiliated with, endorsed, sponsored, or specifically approved by [rights holder], unless a specific license says otherwise.

Replace bracketed language with actual license-required text.

## Credits and copyright page minimum

- Title.
- Edition/version.
- Copyright holder and year.
- Publisher/contact URL.
- Writing/design/layout/editing credits.
- Art/map/icon/font credits.
- License notice and attribution.
- Compatibility disclaimer, if needed.
- Printing/export notes in colophon or production page.
- ISBN, SKU, or product code if applicable.

## Prohibited shortcuts

- Do not copy trade dress from a famous RPG.
- Do not use protected setting names, monsters, logos, or visual identity unless licensed.
- Do not generate fake endorsements or fake playtesters.
- Do not state “all rights cleared” unless the rights ledger supports it.
- Do not assume web images, Pinterest images, screenshots, or fan art are usable.
