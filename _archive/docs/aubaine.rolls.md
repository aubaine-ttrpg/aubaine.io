# Aubaine — Resolution & Difficulty Framework

*A Structural Definition of Uncertainty and Opposition*

Resolution in Aubaine follows a unified mechanical structure. Every roll, regardless of context, is built from the same core components and interpreted through consistent principles of opposition, difficulty, and consequence.

## The Universal Roll

When uncertainty requires resolution, a character rolls:

**1d20 + Ability modifier + Aptitude modifier**

This structure applies to Ability Rolls, Attack Rolls, Saving Rolls, and Opposed Rolls. All fractions in Aubaine are rounded up.

Rolls are only made when the outcome is uncertain and failure carries meaningful consequence. If an action cannot reasonably fail, or if failure would not change the situation, no roll is required. The Game Master determines when a roll is appropriate.

---

## Difficulty Classes

For general Ability Rolls, the Game Master assigns a Difficulty Class (DC) according to narrative difficulty.

| Difficulty      | DC |
| --------------- | -- |
| Very Easy       | 5  |
| Easy            | 10 |
| Medium          | 15 |
| Hard            | 20 |
| Very Hard       | 25 |
| Near Impossible | 30 |

The DC scale represents narrative resistance rather than mathematical expectation. The Game Master selects the value that best reflects the fictional challenge.

For Saving Rolls generated by skills, the standard formula for determining the DC is:

**8 + relevant Ability modifier + relevant Aptitude modifier**

The character resisting the effect rolls **1d20 + Ability modifier + Aptitude modifier** against this DC. In all Saving Rolls, the resisting character is considered the defender.

Difficulty Classes are hidden by default. The Game Master may reveal a DC when doing so enhances tension or clarity.

---

## Armor Class and Attack Rolls

Attack Rolls follow the same universal roll structure. The result is compared against the target’s Armor Class (AC). If the total equals or exceeds the AC, the attack succeeds.

---

## Situation Modifiers

In circumstances where an action becomes more difficult or easier due to specific fictional positioning, the Game Master may apply a Situation Modifier.

Situation Modifiers apply only to rolls with fixed mechanical thresholds — specifically Attack Rolls (modifying Armor Class) and Saving Rolls (modifying Save DC). They are not used to alter general narrative DC assignments.

The following scale serves as a reference:

### Increased Difficulty

| Fictional Adjustment | Modifier |
| -------------------- | -------- |
| Slightly Harder      | +2       |
| Hard                 | +4       |
| Very Hard            | +6       |
| Near Impossible      | +8       |
| Absurd               | +10      |

### Reduced Difficulty

| Fictional Adjustment  | Modifier |
| --------------------- | -------- |
| Slightly Easier       | –2       |
| Favorable             | –4       |
| Major Advantage       | –6       |
| Near Certain          | –8       |
| Essentially Automatic | –10      |

A Situation Modifier reflects precision, leverage, environmental factors, or the complexity of the intended outcome. It is determined by the Game Master in the same manner as narrative DC selection.

For example, targeting a specific limb or weak point may increase the effective Armor Class. Repeated success may alter the fiction and change subsequent modifiers. Likewise, strong positioning or preparation may reduce effective thresholds.

Players are not required to know the numerical value of a Situation Modifier, though increased or reduced difficulty should be communicated narratively.

---

## Opposed Rolls

When multiple parties directly contest one another, each participant rolls using the universal roll structure. The highest total succeeds.

In the event of a tie, the defender prevails. The defender is the party resisting the attempted action.

---

## Critical Results

A natural roll of **1** is always a critical failure. It results in automatic failure regardless of modifiers or thresholds and introduces a negative twist determined by the Game Master.

A natural roll of **20** is always a critical success. It results in automatic success regardless of modifiers or thresholds and applies an additional effect depending on the roll type:

* For Ability Rolls, the player introduces a favorable twist consistent with the fiction.
* For Attack Rolls, damage dice are doubled.
* For Saving Rolls, the roll succeeds automatically without additional effect.
* For Opposed Rolls, a natural 20 results in automatic victory unless multiple participants roll natural 20s. In that case, the highest total succeeds, with the defender prevailing on a tie.

Critical results override all modifiers and difficulty calculations.

---

## Defender Priority

Across all roll types, when totals are equal, the defender prevails. In Saving Rolls, the character resisting the effect is always considered the defender. This rule applies equally to player characters and hostile creatures.
